Innovator

Katherine Johnson

Background

Katherine Johnson was born in White Sulphur Springs, West Virginia, on August 26, 1918. She showed strong mathematical ability from an early age and attended high school and college at West Virginia State College. After a short teaching career, she joined NACA/NASA in 1953 and worked there until 1986, contributing to many early space missions.

As an African American woman working before and during the Civil Rights movement, Johnson faced segregation and discrimination in education and at work. Despite these barriers, she became a leading mathematician at NASA and helped break barriers for women and people of color in STEM.

Contributions

Katherine Johnson performed critical calculations for orbital mechanics, including launch windows and trajectories for the Mercury and Apollo missions. She worked on calculations for Alan Shepard's 1961 flight, John Glenn's 1962 orbit, and the 1969 Apollo 11 moon landing, and contributed to research papers and planning for later programs.
